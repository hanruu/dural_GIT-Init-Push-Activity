SENSORS

A sensor is a device that detects and responds to some type of input from the physical environment. It produces an output signal for the purpose of sensing a physical phenomenon, such as light, heat, motion, moisture, or pressure. Sensors are used in a wide range of applications, from everyday objects like touch-sensitive elevator buttons to industrial and environmental gas analysis. They play a pivotal role in the Internet of Things (IoT) by enabling the collection and processing of data about specific environmental conditions. Sensors can be classified into various types based on the phenomena they detect, such as acoustic, temperature, proximity, and chemical sensors. The "Sensors" journal is an international, peer-reviewed, open access journal that focuses on the science and technology of sensors. It is published semimonthly online and is indexed within various databases, including Scopus, SCIE (Web of Science), PubMed, and others. In summary, sensors are essential devices that enable the detection and measurement of various physical phenomena, playing a critical role in numerous applications and industries.
